Welcome to Ilocos Sur! A land of history, culture, and breathtaking beauty from the cobblestone streets of Vigan to the stunning coastlines and authentic local traditions, Ilocos Sur invites you to discover its timeless charm.

Tourist Spots & Adventures

1. Calle Crisologo

Calle Crisologo, located in the heart of Vigan, Ilocos Sur, is a famous cobblestone street lined with well-preserved Spanish colonial houses. Stretching about 500 meters, it showcases ancestral homes with capiz shell windows, wooden doors, and intricate iron grills that reflect the fusion of Filipino, Chinese, and Spanish architectural influences.

2. Bantay Bell Tower

Bantay Bell Tower provides panoramic views of Vigan and the surrounding fields and coastline. Climb the tower for memorable photos and learn about the site's historical significance. Nearby are small shops selling local snacks and souvenirs.

#BantayBellTower #Viewpoint #History #Photography #IlocosSur

3.Candon City ilocosur

Candon City, known as the “Tobacco Capital of the Philippines,” is a vibrant city in southern Ilocos Sur. It is famous for its vast tobacco plantations, rich cultural traditions, and sweet delicacy called calamay (a sticky rice cake with coconut milk and sugar). The city played a key role in the Philippine Revolution, earning a place in history for the Ikkis ti Candon (Cry of Candon) in 1898, when local revolutionaries rose against Spanish rule.
